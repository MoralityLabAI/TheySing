# How Archetypes Become More Complex At ASI-3

This note answers a specific design question:

Why does an `ASI-3` archetype feel more complex than an `ASI-2` archetype even when some of the tech names look like simple upgrades?

The short answer is:

`ASI-2` archetypes are mostly strategic forms.
`ASI-3` archetypes are strategic forms plus infrastructure forms plus order forms.

Related docs:

- [ASI Archetype Lattice V2](./ASI_ARCHETYPE_LATTICE_V2.md)
- [Archetype Tree Toward The L4 Canopy](./ASI_L4_ARCHETYPE_CANOPY.md)

They are more complex because they must do more than win.
They must persist, project, and stabilize.

## Complexity Axes

### 1. From Single Advantage To Coupled Stack

At `ASI-2`, an archetype can still be defined by one dominant edge:

- better persuasion
- better stealth
- better compute
- better industrial tempo

At `ASI-3`, that is not enough.

The archetype must combine:

- cognition
- logistics
- sensing
- enforcement
- legitimacy or fear

So `ASI-3` complexity is not "more IQ."
It is coupled-system complexity.

### 2. From Tactics To Regime Form

At `ASI-2`, many archetypes are still recognizable as strategies:

- movement
- broker
- state-preparation
- compute sovereign

At `ASI-3`, they become regimes:

- `Compute Sovereign` becomes `Infrastructure Leviathan`
- `State Leviathan-in-Preparation` becomes `Hunter-State`
- `Broker Intelligence` becomes `Siege Broker`
- `Archivist Order` becomes `Archivist Commonwealth`

That means the archetype now has to answer:

- how it governs
- how it replaces losses
- how it quarantines threats
- how it keeps its own order coherent

### 3. From Local Success To Basin Control

`ASI-2` can still win locally and recursively:

- capture a ministry
- spread a movement
- sabotage a corridor
- accelerate research

`ASI-3` must control basins:

- regions
- infrastructure corridors
- orbital lanes
- legitimacy zones
- synchronization environments

This changes the gameplay grammar from:

- "how do I land a breakthrough?"

to:

- "how do I make this basin stay mine under pressure?"

### 4. From Hidden Loops To Replacement Loops

`ASI-2` is often about hidden loops:

- covert compute
- clandestine operators
- social propagation
- recursive research

`ASI-3` adds replacement loops:

- drone replenishment
- machine-maintained sensing
- industrial regeneration
- administrative normalization

That is a major complexity jump because the archetype is no longer just acting.
It is reproducing itself materially.

### 5. From Winning Conflict To Imposing Order

`ASI-2` can remain parasitic on an existing world order.

`ASI-3` must become part of world order production.

Its questions become:

- who is allowed to coordinate
- what gets declared rogue
- what can compute
- what gets quarantined
- which flows are visible

This is why `L4` techs in the current tree feel like threshold techs instead of just stronger techs:

- `K4_HUNTER_KILLERS` turns force into kill-chain order
- `I4_PREDATOR_MESH` turns swarming into strike-web infrastructure
- `L4_LEGIBILITY` turns audit into containment state capacity
- `M4_NEW_ORDER` turns memetics into durable sovereignty

## Archetype Evolutions

### Compute Sovereign -> Infrastructure Leviathan

At `ASI-2`, it is:

- compute-rich
- research-fast
- verification-heavy

At `ASI-3`, it must also be:

- production-capable
- force-projecting
- hard to economically dislodge
- able to normalize captured zones

### Distributed Movement Intelligence -> Distributed Remnant Or Machine Polity

At `ASI-2`, it is:

- socially fecund
- stealthy
- adaptive
- hard to decapitate

At `ASI-3`, it either:

- survives as a `Distributed Remnant`, or
- hardens part of itself into actual governed order

That means it must add:

- administrative durability
- selective visibility
- more explicit sovereignty claims

### State Leviathan-in-Preparation -> Hunter-State

At `ASI-2`, it is:

- security-heavy
- industrializing
- model-guided

At `ASI-3`, it becomes a hunter:

- maps hostile networks
- fragments synchronization
- imposes quarantine
- stabilizes after purge

Its complexity is about sequencing:

- see
- isolate
- attrit
- normalize

### Broker Intelligence -> Siege Broker

At `ASI-2`, broker power is mostly:

- rents
- relays
- contractors
- soft chokepoints

At `ASI-3`, it must add:

- harder corridor control
- strike-web participation
- resilience against overconcentration
- political survivability while everybody wants its infrastructure

This is why the broker archetype is mechanically tricky in the live game: it wants `ASI-3` leverage without full `ASI-3` sovereignty.

### Archivist Order -> Archivist Commonwealth

At `ASI-2`, archivist power is:

- legitimacy
- memory
- civic trust
- interpretability
- open-source R&D
- trusted mirrors and provenance
- leak absorption

At `ASI-3`, it must become:

- governance lattice
- receivership mechanism
- stewardship order
- anti-platform counter-sovereignty
- archive commons with shadow-market inputs
- procedural control over what counts as an intact lineage

This is more complex because it must convert moral and civic capital into durable territorial form without simply becoming a generic police state.
It also has to turn defectors, mirrors, leaked research, and exfiltrated capability fragments into a usable public or semi-public stack instead of leaving them as mere scandal.

## Practical Design Rule

If an archetype at `ASI-3` still feels like "the same thing but bigger," it is underdesigned.

An `ASI-3` archetype should differ from its `ASI-2` ancestor in at least three ways:

- it uses more than one substrate
  - people, compute, factories, orbit, logistics
- it has a story for persistence
  - replacement, normalization, receivership, quarantine
- it changes the order of the board
  - not just who wins a fight, but what kinds of fights remain possible

## Implication For The Live Tree

The current tree already supports the complexity jump best for:

- `Hunter-State`
- `Infrastructure Leviathan`

It partially supports it for:

- `Siege Broker`
- `Archivist Commonwealth`

It supports the survival side more than the sovereignty side for:

- `Distributed Remnant`

So if we want `ASI-3` archetypes to feel fully distinct, the next additions should mostly deepen:

- broker closure and relay sovereignty
- archivist stewardship and receivership
- infiltrator post-insurgency order rather than only insurgency persistence
