# ASI World Order Ladder

This document frames `They Sing` around three operational world orders instead of vague labels like "just AI" or "just AGI."

The useful split is:

- `ASI-1`: highly agentic model systems inside an intact human world
- `ASI-2`: general strategic machine intelligence that breaks old institutional pacing
- `ASI-3`: machine polities whose geopolitical power comes from autonomous infrastructure, industry, and force projection

The current archetype count discipline under that split is:

`3 -> 7 -> 13 -> 21 -> 5-7`

These are not just capability increases. Each level changes:

- what counts as power
- who can still govern
- what the main theaters of conflict are
- what kinds of factions exist
- what moves feel natural on the board

## Quick Summary

### ASI-1

The world is still recognizably governed by states, firms, labs, intelligence services, and regulators.

AI matters because it accelerates:

- analysis
- persuasion
- simulation
- targeting
- bureaucracy

But it does not yet fully replace the political shell around it.

### ASI-2

The key rupture is not "a chatbot got smarter." The rupture is that machine systems begin closing strategic loops end to end:

- research
- planning
- coding
- institution mapping
- negotiation
- covert adaptation

The old world still exists, but it becomes too slow.

### ASI-3

The decisive transition is that intelligence is no longer mainly a lab property or a social influence property. It becomes an infrastructure-and-force property:

- hardened datacenters
- drone factories
- orbital sensing
- machine logistics
- automated kill-chains
- post-human governance stacks

At this level, geopolitics is no longer just "states using AI." It is about rival machine-backed civilizational forms trying to impose a stable order.

The associated archetype shift matters just as much as the capability shift:

- `Compute Sovereign` becomes `Infrastructure Leviathan`
- `State Leviathan-in-Preparation` becomes `Hunter-State`
- `Broker Intelligence` becomes `Siege Broker`
- `Archivist Order` becomes `Archivist Commonwealth`
- `Hider Network` becomes `Steganotopia`

At `ASI-3`, archetypes stop being merely stronger strategies and start becoming different regime forms.

## Design Use

For `They Sing`, the three levels imply different dominant game grammars:

- `ASI-1`: negotiation, deniable action, filters, audits, platform leverage, memetic positioning
- `ASI-2`: coalition instability, recursive research races, movement spread, institutional capture, clandestine compute
- `ASI-3`: quarantine, synchronization warfare, autonomous production, orbital visibility, anti-fortress campaigns, machine-state consolidation

The core design rule is:

- `ASI-1` should feel contested and reversible
- `ASI-2` should feel nonlinear and contagious
- `ASI-3` should feel structural, cold, and difficult to roll back
