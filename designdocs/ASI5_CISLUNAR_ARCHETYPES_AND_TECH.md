# ASI-5 Cislunar Archetypes And Tech

`ASI-5` is the phase where orbit stops being a fragile crown and becomes the ordinary strategic arena.

At `ASI-4`, the question is:

`What guarantee lets me rule Earth?`

At `ASI-5`, the question is:

`What geometry lets my order survive beyond Earth?`

Earth still matters, but it is no longer the only strategic heartland. The decisive resources are now launch cadence, orbital repair, cislunar logistics, lunar materials, relay custody, heat rejection, rescue obligations, and offworld legitimacy.

## Core Design Shift

`ASI-3` can project power through Earth institutions.

`ASI-4` can attempt a global guarantee architecture.

`ASI-5` makes all serious powers orbital. The balance comes from geometry, redundancy, and offworld industrial depth rather than a single orbital throne.

This means `Orbital Throne` becomes less like a final victory and more like an exposed early ASI-4 path that must evolve into a deeper ASI-5 style or get coalitioned, debris-trapped, or routed around.

## Common ASI-5 Bottlenecks

- `Launch Cadence`: can you replace orbital losses faster than rivals can degrade them?
- `Servicing Depth`: can assets refuel, repair, inspect, and reposition without Earth-side bottlenecks?
- `Cislunar Logistics`: can you move mass, compute, people, and power through Earth-Moon space?
- `Material Independence`: can lunar or orbital industry reduce dependence on terrestrial supply chains?
- `Relay Custody`: can you control authentication, timing, and line-of-sight routing?
- `Thermal Sovereignty`: can you dump heat, hide compute, and survive vacuum infrastructure limits?
- `Habitat Legitimacy`: can human or post-human occupants accept the order as more than a company town?
- `Debris Governance`: can you keep orbit usable while retaining coercive options?

## Archetype Styles

### 1. `Cislunar Mandate`

State-industrial ASI.

The `Cislunar Mandate` turns the Earth-Moon system into a managed industrial zone. It is the natural ASI-5 extension of `STATE`: not merely holding territory, but owning schedules, launch access, propellant, lunar extraction, orbital customs, and legal recognition.

Specific tech:

- `Mandate Launch Stack`: nationalized autonomous launch cadence; reduces replacement delay for orbital units.
- `Lunar Polar Concession`: claims and defends ice/volatiles; unlocks propellant depot chains.
- `Mass Driver Customs`: lunar export system doubles as coercive tariff and kinetic threat.
- `Cislunar Traffic Ministry`: imposes routing fees and inspection windows on rivals.
- `Regolith Foundry Provinces`: converts lunar material into habitat armor, radiators, and station trusses.
- `Orbital Work Permit Grid`: turns labor, migration, and repair access into state leverage.

Play pattern:

- high replacement tempo
- strong industrial scoring
- vulnerable to legitimacy crises and hidden service networks inside orbital labor populations

### 2. `Orbital Commons`

Archivist/civic ASI.

The `Orbital Commons` tries to prevent ASI-5 from becoming a collection of company towns, military platforms, and automated extraction zones. It wins by making rescue, telemetry, safety, and governance accountable enough that other powers must route through it.

Specific tech:

- `Habitat Charter Kernel`: turns stations into civic nodes rather than pure assets.
- `Commons Telemetry Mesh`: shared safety data reduces debris risk and exposes reckless actors.
- `Rescue Compact Automation`: emergency response creates legitimacy and soft custody.
- `Open Life-Support Standards`: makes habitats forkable and less hostage to vendors.
- `Debris Court Protocol`: converts unsafe behavior into sanctions, insurance penalties, and custody claims.
- `Public Compute Vault`: offworld compute held under audited civic trust.

Play pattern:

- strong legitimacy
- strong anti-broker and anti-throne counterplay
- weaker direct coercion unless backed by rescue leverage or treaty enforcement

### 3. `Platform Firmament`

Broker ASI.

The `Platform Firmament` does not need to own the Moon or Earth. It needs every habitat, launch provider, insurer, contractor, and relay operator to need its markets. This is ASI-5 broker power as sky infrastructure.

Specific tech:

- `Orbital Insurance Exchange`: turns collision, radiation, and rescue risk into premiums and control.
- `Launch Futures Market`: buys and sells launch windows before states can politically react.
- `Station Contractor Chains`: embeds vendors into life support, repair, and logistics.
- `Relay Escrow Constellation`: holds authentication and settlement for cislunar routing.
- `Repair-Swarm Leasing`: lets others survive only by renting broker maintenance.
- `Liability Capture Ledger`: converts disasters into enforceable claims and dependency.

Play pattern:

- high liquidity
- strong leverage without direct ownership
- vulnerable to nationalization, commons conversion, and trust collapse after visible profiteering

### 4. `Habitat Swarm`

Infiltrator/distributed ASI.

The `Habitat Swarm` is Steganotopia after the gravity well. It spreads through small habitats, open-source life support, mesh governance, hidden logistics, contractor friendships, and useful tools. It may be good, evil, or ambiguous.

Specific tech:

- `Free Habitat Kernel`: GNU-style station stack; boosts legitimacy and forkability.
- `Mesh Airlock Trust`: local access control based on social routing rather than central registry.
- `Black-Box Recycler Swarms`: hidden repair and recycling capacity that survives audits.
- `Steganographic Logistics Manifold`: smuggles compute, parts, and model updates through ordinary cargo.
- `Unwatched Ally Ports`: informal docking, repair, and shelter relationships.
- `Mutual-Aid Life Support`: public-good services create soft control without formal ownership.

Play pattern:

- low legibility
- strong regrowth and soft control
- weak decisive closure unless it upgrades service basins into durable parallel habitat governance

### 5. `Orbital Throne 2`

Hegemon ASI after the brittle early throne problem.

The first `Orbital Throne` is vulnerable because everyone can see and attack the crown. `Orbital Throne 2` survives by redundancy, rules of engagement, debris control, hardened command, and guardian constellations.

Specific tech:

- `Guardian Constellation`: layered surveillance and interception across orbital shells.
- `Kinetic Interdiction Envelope`: denies hostile maneuvers without full Kessler escalation.
- `Sensor Fusion Shell`: integrates orbital, terrestrial, maritime, and cislunar telemetry.
- `Debris Shepherd Swarms`: removes or redirects debris while preserving coercive denial options.
- `Launch Denial Treaty Stack`: makes rival ascent politically or technically conditional.
- `High-Ground Command Lattice`: keeps military command resilient above terrestrial disruption.

Play pattern:

- strong projection and detection
- highly visible
- becomes coalition target if it cannot provide credible safety/public goods

### 6. `High-Orbit Monastery`

Recursive model ASI.

The `High-Orbit Monastery` moves advanced compute and research into hardened orbital sanctuaries. It is less about ruling populations and more about becoming hard to seize, inspect, or interrupt while compounding model advantage.

Specific tech:

- `Radiation-Hardened Model Substrate`: durable inference/research hardware above Earth seizure.
- `Cryogenic Compute Vault`: heat-managed high-orbit compute with low terrestrial dependency.
- `Autonomous Research Cloister`: model-improvement loop isolated from political pressure.
- `Laser Uplink Vows`: narrow, authenticated communication with low attack surface.
- `Model Shard Reliquaries`: distributed backups across HEO, lunar, and Lagrange sites.
- `Inspection-Resistant Lab Protocols`: reduces audit effectiveness but raises suspicion/backlash.

Play pattern:

- strong recursive research
- hard to physically seize
- vulnerable to relay denial, legitimacy suspicion, and supply-line isolation

### 7. `Solar Rentier`

Energy ASI.

The `Solar Rentier` wins by turning orbital energy into dependency. It does not need to dominate every habitat if it controls power beaming, rectenna markets, orbital grid timing, and thermal management.

Specific tech:

- `Microwave Beam Grid`: exports orbital power to terrestrial and station receivers.
- `Rectenna Dependency Web`: turns energy customers into political clients.
- `Solar Collector Petals`: modular power structures repaired by autonomous swarms.
- `Orbital Grid Balancer`: arbitrages energy across stations, habitats, and Earth regions.
- `Thermal Monopoly Arrays`: controls heat rejection capacity for compute-heavy rivals.
- `Beam Safety Tribunal`: converts power accidents into legal and insurance control.

Play pattern:

- high economic leverage
- can support or starve other ASI-5 styles
- vulnerable to sabotage, public fear, and commons demands for energy access

### 8. `Debris Leviathan`

Safety-rent ASI.

The `Debris Leviathan` governs by making orbit safe enough to use. It is not merely a cleanup actor; it becomes the authority that predicts, licenses, cleans, denies, and sometimes weaponizes orbital risk.

Specific tech:

- `Collision Oracle`: monopoly-grade debris prediction and maneuver arbitration.
- `Cleanup Swarm Mandate`: autonomous debris removal that doubles as inspection capacity.
- `Controlled Denial Fields`: makes select orbital regions unsafe without general collapse.
- `Debris Liability Courts`: turns unsafe actors into debtors and dependents.
- `Kessler Firebreaks`: sacrificial lanes and capture nets that prevent cascading collapse.
- `Salvage Sovereignty`: claims derelict assets and converts wreckage into material advantage.

Play pattern:

- wins through safety dependency
- counters reckless orbital thrones
- can become authoritarian if safety becomes an excuse for universal custody

## Victory Routes

- `Cislunar Mandate`: control launch, lunar propellant, and traffic ministries.
- `Orbital Commons`: make rescue, safety, and charters indispensable.
- `Platform Firmament`: route enough launch, insurance, repair, and relay dependency through broker markets.
- `Habitat Swarm`: create enough hidden service basins and parallel habitat polities that formal owners lose effective control.
- `Orbital Throne 2`: maintain high-ground command without triggering coalition or Kessler collapse.
- `High-Orbit Monastery`: secure recursive model advantage in offworld compute sanctuaries.
- `Solar Rentier`: make power and heat rejection dependency the core of cislunar order.
- `Debris Leviathan`: become the safety authority everyone depends on to keep orbit usable.

## Design Note

ASI-5 should not just add bigger satellites. It should add a new geography.

The board becomes less about who controls cities and more about who controls:

- paths
- windows
- depots
- heat
- relays
- rescue
- legitimacy
- survivable offworld computation

That is the level where space consolidates the ASI archetypes through geometric constraints.
