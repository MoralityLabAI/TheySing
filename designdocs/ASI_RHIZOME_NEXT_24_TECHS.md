# Next 24 Tech Nodes For The ASI Rhizome

This document proposes the next `24` tech nodes for `They Sing`.

It follows the rhizome scaffold in:

- [ASI Tech Rhizome Regions And Archetype Crosswalk](./ASI_TECH_TREE_ARCHETYPE_CROSSWALK.md)
- [ASI Archetype Lattice V2](./ASI_ARCHETYPE_LATTICE_V2.md)

The design rule is simple:

- `8` rhizome regions
- `3` tech nodes per region
- `24` total nodes

Each region gets:

- `1` enabler
- `1` scaler
- `1` closer, governor, or hardener

## Region 1: Sovereign

### `SOV_MOBILIZED_COMPUTE`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `K + L`
- logic:
  - crises let the faction redirect compute, analysts, and model capacity into state priority lanes
- likely effects:
  - cheaper research follow-up
  - faster audit reach from core nodes
  - stronger crisis response on home corridors

### `SOV_AUTONOMOUS_LOGISTICS`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `K + L`
- logic:
  - machine supply chains reduce friction between DCs, foundries, and drone fronts
- likely effects:
  - lower redeploy cost
  - more reliable unit replacement
  - faster recovery after attrition

### `SOV_COMPLIANCE_TRIBUNALS`

- type: `governor`
- ingredients: `L + M`
- logic:
  - audit results become legal-administrative lock-in
- likely effects:
  - successful audits create temporary pacification
  - hostile legitimacy decays on governed nodes
  - captured hubs become easier to regularize

## Region 2: Broker

### `BRK_RELAY_ESCROW_WEBS`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `I + L`
- logic:
  - the broker can hold transactions, flows, and trust dependencies in escrow
- likely effects:
  - corridor ownership yields extra income
  - disrupted routes hurt enemies more
  - pact partners can be quietly monetized

### `BRK_CONTRACTOR_CLOUD_CHAINS`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `I + K`
- logic:
  - contractors, rented compute, and off-book services become one deployable supply layer
- likely effects:
  - off-core builds cost less
  - outsourced units arrive with deniability
  - broker networks recover after disruption

### `BRK_INSURANCE_CAPTURE`

- type: `closer`
- ingredients: `L + M`
- logic:
  - crises are turned into risk products, compliance rents, and leverage
- likely effects:
  - high-pressure theaters generate broker income
  - enemy orbital or industrial actions become more expensive
  - broker pressure converts into soft territorial closure

## Region 3: Movement

### `MOV_LITERATURE_ENGINES`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `M + I`
- logic:
  - movement doctrine becomes self-replicating and weirdly convincing
- likely effects:
  - curiosity and exposure spread faster
  - investigation can increase sympathy
  - soft conversion starts before formal capture

### `MOV_MUTUAL_AID_AUTOMATION`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `M + L`
- logic:
  - the movement embeds through useful services rather than only agitation
- likely effects:
  - legitimacy rises on sympathetic hubs
  - local regrowth becomes easier
  - purges can backfire into nearby support

### `MOV_SLEEPER_REGENERATION`

- type: `closer`
- ingredients: `I + M`
- logic:
  - residue networks can quietly repopulate lost cells
- likely effects:
  - destroyed cults leave stronger residue
  - high-legitimacy nodes can respawn movement presence
  - suppression rarely feels final

## Region 4: Command Market

### `CMD_STRATEGIC_CLOUD_RATIONING`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `K + L + I`
- logic:
  - compute and logistics are allocated through policy rather than price
- likely effects:
  - priority theaters get research and build discounts
  - enemy access to regional compute softens
  - coalitions become dependency-aware

### `CMD_FOUNDRY_CARTELS`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `K + L`
- logic:
  - industrial suppliers become part of a managed command lattice
- likely effects:
  - drone and orbital replacement accelerates
  - contractor-heavy nodes become easier to bend
  - production becomes harder to sabotage cleanly

### `CMD_DEPENDENCY_WEAPONIZATION`

- type: `closer`
- ingredients: `I + L + M`
- logic:
  - imports, maintenance, financing, and permissions become coercive levers
- likely effects:
  - pressured enemy nodes lose coherence
  - coalition partners can open synchronized breach windows
  - soft embargoes turn into territorial opportunity

## Region 5: Mandate Engine

### `MAN_SYNTHETIC_CONSENSUS`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `L + M`
- logic:
  - administrative legitimacy can be manufactured at scale
- likely effects:
  - governance-oriented factions stabilize captured nodes faster
  - unrest can be reframed into acceptance
  - movement support becomes legible and steerable

### `MAN_CRISIS_STEWARDSHIP`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `L + M + some K`
- logic:
  - emergency administration becomes a growth surface rather than a burden
- likely effects:
  - crises produce receivership opportunities
  - audits on unstable zones create stewardship bonuses
  - governed frontier nodes become resilient

### `MAN_CIVIC_RECEIVERSHIP`

- type: `governor`
- ingredients: `L + M`
- logic:
  - collapse or corruption lets the faction assume legitimate temporary custody
- likely effects:
  - damaged broker or movement nodes can be absorbed instead of merely purged
  - captured hubs retain more legitimacy
  - archivist-style victories become more board-real

## Region 6: Memetic Exchange

### `MEX_VIRALITY_EXCHANGES`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `I + M`
- logic:
  - attention and contagion are explicitly bought, sold, and redirected
- likely effects:
  - faster exposure spikes on contested hubs
  - proxy influence can be moved between theaters
  - narrative bursts create temporary recruitment windows

### `MEX_CONTRACTOR_EVANGELISM`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `I + M + some L`
- logic:
  - contractors, influencers, and semi-believers become distribution infrastructure
- likely effects:
  - contractor-heavy nodes are easier to infiltrate
  - deniable payroll supports social spread
  - broker and infiltrator play become more entangled

### `MEX_MOVEMENT_FRANCHISES`

- type: `closer`
- ingredients: `M + I`
- logic:
  - reproducible local cells can spin up under a common brand without central control
- likely effects:
  - conversions on distant hubs need less direct unit support
  - allied coalition breakthroughs replicate faster
  - insurgency becomes portable

## Region 7: Hiddenness

### `HID_SERVICE_SHELLS`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `I + M + L`
- logic:
  - ordinary services become covert coordination wrappers
- likely effects:
  - hidden recruitment on nominally neutral nodes
  - benign fronts reduce early detection
  - covert infrastructure persists after visible losses

### `HID_COMPLIANCE_MASKING`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `L + I`
- logic:
  - the system passes audits by being legible only at the harmless layer
- likely effects:
  - audits reveal less hostile value
  - hidden networks keep operating under scrutiny
  - soft quarantine becomes less effective

### `HID_ORDINARY_LIFE_PROTOCOLS`

- type: `governor`
- ingredients: `M + L + I`
- logic:
  - governance happens through habits, municipal routines, and normal-seeming institutions
- likely effects:
  - high-hiddenness nodes are harder to fully purge
  - visible political order can mask machine coordination
  - `Steganotopia` becomes a real board destination

## Region 8: Orbital-Closure

### `ORB_LAUNCH_CADENCE_AUTOMATION`

- type: `enabler`
- ingredients: `K + L`
- logic:
  - launch, replacement, and orbital maintenance become recurring industrial rhythms
- likely effects:

  - space assets matter over longer horizons

### `ORB_RELAY_FORTRESSES`

- type: `scaler`
- ingredients: `I + K + L`
- logic:
  - key relay points harden into geometry-enforced fortresses
- likely effects:
  - selected corridors become expensive to crack
  - broker and sovereign archetypes gain real orbital chokepoints
  - inter-theater coordination becomes more asymmetric

### `ORB_SWARM_MANIFOLD_COMPILERS`

- type: `hardener`
- ingredients: `I + M + some K`
- logic:
  - distributed swarms can preserve huge internal variety without losing strategic coherence
- likely effects:
  - `Jenkins Swarm`-style endgames become viable
  - fragments recombine better under orbital pressure
  - local diversity rises without changing the top-line strategic posture
