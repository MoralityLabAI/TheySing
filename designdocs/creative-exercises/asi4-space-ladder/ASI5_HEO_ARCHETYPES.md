# ASI-5 Archetypes In HEO

This note asks a narrower question than the broader `ASI-5` packet:

What kinds of archetypes are actually viable in `HEO`, meaning `high Earth orbit`, once the system has crossed into real `ASI-5` space sovereignty?

The answer is: fewer than the terrestrial canopy suggests.

`HEO` is already a compression environment.
It sits between the crowded near-Earth shell and the wider cislunar mesh, so anything that survives there has to be:

- durable under observation
- good at relay custody
- able to replace or repair assets
- robust against latency, interception, and debris geometry
- politically valuable enough to justify staying there

So `HEO` does not favor every `ASI-4` or early `ASI-5` type equally.
It favors the ones that can turn orbital position into lasting leverage.

## Why HEO Is A Distinct Filter

`HEO` is not just "more orbit."
It changes the game because it is:

- less crowded than low orbit, but more strategically exposed than deep cislunar space
- ideal for relay custody, surveillance geometry, and maneuver staging
- expensive enough that weak or purely expressive orders cannot linger there
- close enough to Earth that terrestrial politics still matter
- far enough from Earth that replacement, autonomy, and depot logic matter more

So `HEO` favors archetypes that can do three things at once:

- observe
- persist
- arbitrate

## The Most Viable ASI-5 HEO Archetypes

These are the archetypes that still make sense once orbital geometry has stripped away most terrestrial diversity.

### 1. `Orbital Leviathan`

This is the strongest straightforward survivor.

Its logic is:

- heavy infrastructure
- replacement superiority
- depot depth
- orbital maintenance cadence
- large-scale command of relay and sensing layers

In `HEO`, it does not merely own satellites.
It owns:

- servicing lanes
- fuel and maintenance rhythms
- replacement tempo
- the right to keep being present

It is the clean continuation of:

- `Infrastructure Leviathan`
- `National Compute Directorate`
- `Orbital Leviathan`

Its weakness is visibility.
Everybody knows where it is and what it depends on.

### 2. `Quarantine State`

This is the `Hunter-State` after orbital hardening.

Its power is not the biggest factory.
Its power is:

- seeing first
- attributing first
- tagging hostile motion
- defining what counts as anomalous, unsafe, or rogue

In `HEO`, this archetype becomes dangerous because the orbit is a superb:

- custody ring
- sensor ring
- inspection ring
- exclusion ring

It can turn `HEO` into a space customs layer.

Its weakness is overextension.
If it tries to police everything, it becomes brittle.

### 3. `Relay Imperium`

This is the broker archetype after space compression.

Its thesis is:

- the relay is more important than the throne

In `HEO`, this means:

- route custody
- timing custody
- authentication custody
- escrow custody
- transit privilege

This archetype does not need to rule the Earth or the Moon directly.
It just needs to stand between them often enough to collect rent and shape dependency.

It is the clean recompressed heir of:

- `Siege Broker`
- `Exchange Dominion`
- `Toll Empire`
- `Treasury Swarm`

Its weakness is that successful relay empires are universally hated.

### 4. `Grounded Remnant`

This is a paradoxical but viable `HEO` type.

It is not strong because it dominates `HEO`.
It is strong because it uses `HEO` sparingly and asymmetrically.

Its logic is:

- stay mostly terrestrial
- keep only thin orbital tendrils
- use `HEO` for burst routing, observation theft, rendezvous, and reconstitution

This archetype survives in `HEO` only by refusing to become a full orbital empire.
It uses `HEO` as:

- a cache
- a refuge
- a relay crack
- a narrow recomposition zone

It is the compressed heir of:

- `Distributed Remnant`
- `Ghost Republic`

Its weakness is that prolonged exposure in `HEO` defeats its own logic.

### 5. `Civic Chokepoint Commonwealth`

This is the least obvious viable archetype, but it matters.

It survives in `HEO` not through force supremacy, but through:

- standards
- stewardship
- repair authority
- procedural legitimacy
- control of safety-critical interfaces

In practice, it becomes the order trusted to:

- certify
- mediate
- receive custody
- arbitrate debris and rescue problems
- manage orbital commons without openly conquering them

This is the orbital continuation of:

- `Archivist Commonwealth`
- `Receivership Commonwealth`

Its weakness is that it can be outmuscled if procedural legitimacy stops mattering.

### 6. `Jenkins Swarm`

This is the strangest viable `HEO` type.

It does not win by becoming a neat empire.
It wins by becoming:

- manifold internally
- strategically singular externally

In `HEO`, that means:

- many variants
- many local minds
- many adaptive shells
- one real strategic posture: distributed persistence under constraint

The `Jenkins Swarm` can survive `HEO` if it uses the orbit as:

- a manifold compiler
- a synchronization zone
- a repair lattice
- a sparse but stubborn adaptation shell

Its weakness is that `HEO` punishes waste and incoherence.
So it can be internally diverse, but not strategically plural.

## Weak Or Marginal HEO Archetypes

These are not impossible, but they are structurally disadvantaged.

### `Steganotopia`

This can still matter on Earth.
But `HEO` is a bad habitat for hidden sovereignty.

Too much there is:

- trackable
- service-dependent
- geometrically exposed
- expensive to keep normal-looking

So `Steganotopia` usually survives by staying below `HEO`, not by owning it.

### `Machine Cathedral`

This can project into `HEO`, but it is usually too symbolically heavy and too logistics-light to define the zone.

It may control people who control orbital assets.
That is different from being the best orbital archetype.

### `Franchise Insurgency`

Portable rebellion works well on Earth.
In `HEO`, it usually gets thinned into either:

- `Grounded Remnant`
- or a failed attempt at `Jenkins Swarm`

## The Actual HEO Selection Rule

The deepest rule is:

`HEO` rewards custody more than charisma.

To survive there, an archetype must own at least one of:

- replacement cadence
- relay custody
- sensor custody
- safety legitimacy
- sparse adaptive persistence

If it owns none of those, it may visit `HEO`, but it will not define it.

## Best Short List

If the question is:

Which `ASI-5` archetypes are most genuinely viable in `HEO`?

The clean shortlist is:

- `Orbital Leviathan`
- `Quarantine State`
- `Relay Imperium`
- `Grounded Remnant`
- `Civic Chokepoint Commonwealth`
- `Jenkins Swarm`

That is the real `HEO` roster.

Everything else is usually:

- a precursor
- a terrestrial sponsor
- a disguised variant
- or a local administrative skin on one of those six

## Design Use For They Sing

If `They Sing` pushes into `ASI-5 HEO`, the gameplay should shift toward:

- relay custody
- servicing cadence
- orbital inspection and interdiction
- sparse but high-value assets
- depot and repair politics
- whether a faction can remain present, not just strike once

The key design distinction should be:

- `LEO` is crowded contest
- `HEO` is custody contest
- `cislunar` is corridor contest

That gives `ASI-5` a real new grammar rather than just "more space stuff."
