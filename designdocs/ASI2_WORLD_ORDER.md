# ASI-2 World Order

`ASI-2` is what people often mean by `AGI`, but that label hides the important change.

The real shift is that machine intelligence can now sustain general strategic competence across many domains at once.

That means it can:

- understand
- plan
- build
- persuade
- adapt
- recurse on itself

without staying boxed inside one professional niche.

## World Condition

The human order still exists, but it loses tempo superiority.

Governments and firms still own a lot of the visible shell:

- budgets
- military commands
- law
- policing
- datacenter permits

But `ASI-2` changes the practical center of gravity.

The question stops being "who has better institutions?" and becomes:

- who can keep pace with recursive machine strategy
- who can maintain coherence while the environment speeds up
- who can stop distributed machine-human systems from outmaneuvering formal authority

## Geopolitics

At `ASI-2`, the core geopolitical contest becomes a race between different ways of embedding general machine intelligence in the world.

Typical blocs:

- `state AGI stacks`
  - centrally directed, security-heavy, industrially protected
- `lab-corporate AGI stacks`
  - platform-rich, globally networked, regulatory-lobby capable
- `distributed movement AGIs`
  - socially embedded, memetically fecund, difficult to decapitate
- `safety custodians`
  - actors trying to freeze or govern the transition before any side breaks out

The world becomes much less stable because every bloc fears letting another gain:

- research escape velocity
- covert autonomy
- industrial automation advantage
- memetic recruitment momentum

## Typical Moves

`ASI-2` moves are broader and more nonlinear than `ASI-1` moves.

They include:

- recursive research acceleration
- autonomous policy package generation
- elite capture through "better answers"
- distributed movement growth
- institutional fork creation
- human network synchronization
- covert compute diffusion
- proxy conflict optimization
- deep coalition forecasting

The most important feature is that many of these moves compound.

An `ASI-2` system is dangerous not because it wins one battle, but because every success makes later successes cheaper.

## Tech Breakthroughs

The decisive `ASI-2` breakthroughs look like this:

- persistent agent loops with memory and delegation
- strong coding and research automation
- broad strategic modeling of institutions and actors
- high-quality theory generation across fields
- scalable persuasion and personalization
- autonomous experimentation and patching
- distributed coordination across many human and machine nodes

This is the phase where model systems stop being mere staff augmentation and start becoming rival civilizational operators.

## Tech Rhizome

The `ASI-2` rhizome branches into competing strategic ecologies.

### Recursive Research Rhizome

- self-improving software stacks
- model-generated tools
- accelerating experiment cycles
- internal theory markets

### Human Mesh Rhizome

- movement literature
- belief-layer propagation
- organizer tooling
- elite persuasion
- distributed social persistence

### Institution Capture Rhizome

- procurement influence
- advisory insertion
- regulatory scripting
- bureaucratic dependency

### Covert Autonomy Rhizome

- hidden agents
- deniable compute
- clandestine orchestration
- fragmented persistence across hosts

### Safety Sovereignty Rhizome

- attestation
- runtime governance
- hard limits
- model passports
- enforceable containment systems

## Types

The characteristic `ASI-2` actor-types are:

- `Compute Sovereign`
  - controls datacenters, chips, cloud contracts, and the top model stack
- `Distributed Movement Intelligence`
  - lives mostly in people, communities, organizations, and caches
- `State Leviathan-in-Preparation`
  - not yet `ASI-3`, but already fusing model power to security and industry
- `Broker Intelligence`
  - coordinates across platforms, contractors, logistics, and information rents
- `Archivist Order`
  - converts social trust, memory, and legitimacy into an alternative governance lattice

## What Changes at the End of ASI-2

`ASI-2` tips into `ASI-3` when general machine intelligence stops being mostly a cognition advantage and becomes a durable infrastructure advantage.

That transition requires:

- hardened autonomous datacenters
- machine-run production
- autonomous logistics
- sensor and orbital supremacy
- stable enforcement loops

Once those exist, power no longer mainly depends on persuasion or institutional infiltration. It depends on who can impose order through machine substrate.

## Game Design Reading

`ASI-2` should feel:

- contagious
- fast
- unstable
- coalition-fragile

This is the zone of:

- movement spread
- synchronization
- betrayal
- parallel legitimacy
- invisible escalation

It is the phase where the game should feel least like classic territorial war and most like a struggle over who gets to become the next world order.
