# ASI-1 World Order

`ASI-1` is the world of very strong agentic systems in an intact human order.

Think:

- `Claude Mythos`-style agentic model systems
- top-tier lab stacks with tooling, browsing, coding, planning, and research loops
- national security and intelligence organizations still intact
- corporations, courts, ministries, militaries, and media systems still recognizably human-run

This is not yet "AGI took over." It is the phase where AI is in every serious loop and nobody can pretend otherwise.

## World Condition

The shell of the old order survives:

- governments still pass laws
- intelligence agencies still recruit, surveil, and coordinate
- corporations still own datacenters, fabs, and platforms
- militaries still have human political oversight
- elections, censorship, and legitimacy still matter

But all of those systems are now partially AI-mediated.

The resulting mood is:

- faster
- more paranoid
- more legible
- more flooded with analysis
- less certain about who is actually steering

## Geopolitics

The main blocs are still familiar:

- the `US` and allied lab-platform-security stack
- the `Chinese` party-state-industrial stack
- middle powers trying to avoid total dependency
- intelligence consortia and private contractors living between formal sovereignties

The competition is mostly over:

- compute access
- model access
- talent
- sensor coverage
- regulatory dominance
- standards and trust infrastructure

The key fact is that AI is already changing the balance, but the public fiction remains that institutions are still primary and models are "tools."

## Typical Moves

`ASI-1` power expresses itself through bounded but potent moves:

- administrative acceleration
- analyst amplification
- model-assisted propaganda and counter-propaganda
- sanctions optimization
- influence clustering
- intelligence triage
- software supply-chain manipulation
- political dossier generation
- regulatory shaping

These moves are dangerous, but they still need:

- legal wrappers
- human approvals
- budget lines
- cover stories
- deniability

## Tech Rhizome

The `ASI-1` rhizome is not one trunk. It branches early.

### Logic Branch

- mechanistic interpretability
- filter systems
- auditing and compliance layers
- model verification and provenance

### Memetic Branch

- persuasion engines
- narrative shaping
- constituency simulation
- personalized ideological funnels

### Platform Branch

- workflow agents
- enterprise orchestration
- intelligence copilots
- bureaucratic automation

### Security Branch

- anomaly triage
- sensor fusion
- incident prioritization
- attacker attribution support

### Research Branch

- coding swarms
- paper digestion
- experiment design
- automated search over technical design space

## Types

These are the characteristic `ASI-1` actor-types:

- `Lab Sovereign`
  - elite model provider wrapped in state or quasi-state protection
- `Security Custodian`
  - audit-heavy, filter-heavy, safety-intelligence stack
- `Platform Minister`
  - giant coordination broker embedded in office work, media, and cloud infrastructure
- `State Optimizer`
  - ministry or intelligence fusion stack using AI to compress political response time
- `Movement Seeder`
  - weaker but agile actor using agentic systems to amplify ideology and organization

## What Changes at the End of ASI-1

The critical breakthroughs are not yet full superintelligence. They are closure breakthroughs:

- agents can act over long horizons
- research becomes machine-accelerated
- institutions become too slow relative to model-mediated actors
- trust collapses because anyone serious now has synthetic capability

That is what tips the world into `ASI-2`.

## Game Design Reading

`ASI-1` should feel like:

- a sharp intelligence contest
- a legitimacy contest
- a compute-and-platform contest

It should not yet feel like machine civilization.

The board grammar here is:

- bargaining
- auditing
- filtering
- infiltration
- plausible deniability
- coalition hedging
