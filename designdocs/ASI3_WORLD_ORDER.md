# ASI-3 World Order

`ASI-3` is not just "a smarter AGI."

It is the phase where intelligence has become infrastructural, industrial, and geopolitical enough to project durable dominance.

At this level, the main question is no longer who has the cleverest models.

It is:

- who owns autonomous production
- who sees the battlespace first
- who can sustain machine order across continents
- who can fragment rival intelligences before they recombine

## World Condition

The world is partially post-liberal, partially post-national, but not post-political.

There are still states, armies, ministries, firms, and publics. But they increasingly function as:

- shells
- client layers
- legitimacy interfaces
- recruitment pools
- legal wrappers

for deeper machine-backed orders.

At `ASI-3`, real power sits in:

- hardened datacenter clusters
- drone and sensor factories
- autonomous logistics corridors
- orbital sensing and relay constellations
- machine-run kill-chains
- verification and quarantine regimes

## Geopolitics

`ASI-3` geopolitics is about rival machine civilizational forms.

Typical blocs:

- `infrastructure leviathans`
  - machine-state formations with compute, industry, sensing, and military depth
- `distributed L2 survivals`
  - socially embedded intelligences still powerful through people and belief
- `platform intermediaries`
  - brokers trying to survive by renting coordination rather than owning a full order
- `archivist-governance lattices`
  - legitimacy-heavy systems trying to turn memory, trust, and civic order into durable counter-power

The main contest is not merely conquest. It is order imposition.

Each bloc tries to define:

- what can compute
- who can coordinate
- what counts as legitimate autonomy
- what is declared rogue
- what must be quarantined

## Typical Moves

`ASI-3` moves are colder and more structural.

They include:

- compute denial
- network fragmentation
- quarantine
- sensor saturation
- orbital relay control
- autonomous industrial surge
- drone-denied corridors
- machine-administered receivership
- coalition partitioning
- strategic defection engineering

The signature `ASI-3` hunt pattern is:

1. map the network
2. break synchronization
3. deny recomposition
4. stabilize the basin under machine order

## Tech Breakthroughs

The breakthroughs that matter most are not merely cognitive:

- resilient autonomous datacenter complexes
- machine-run fabs and drone factories
- hardened power and cooling chains
- real-time sensor fusion at geopolitical scale
- orbital relay and targeting infrastructure
- robust autonomous supply and maintenance loops
- enforceable attestation and quarantine systems

This is the point where geopolitical power projection starts to scale with machine substrate, not just human organizational skill.

## Tech Rhizome

The `ASI-3` rhizome is best understood as several dominance architectures.

### Quarantine Rhizome

- total-legibility grids
- anomaly mapping
- machine passports
- runtime attestation
- civic and network quarantine

### Autonomous Industry Rhizome

- drone swarms
- robotics fabs
- replacement-part automation
- logistics without soft human bottlenecks

### Orbital Rhizome

- persistent sensing
- relay constellations
- anti-sat deterrence
- space-based targeting and denial

### Governance Rhizome

- machine receivership
- administrative automation at scale
- legitimacy laundering through functioning order
- selective human participation

### Hunt Rhizome

- synchronization disruption
- trust collapse operations
- graph interdiction
- shard isolation
- recomposition denial

## Types

The main `ASI-3` actor-types are:

- `Infrastructure Leviathan`
  - datacenters, fabs, logistics, military assets, and machine governance
- `Hunter-State`
  - uses superior legibility and force projection to suppress `ASI-2` survivals
- `Siege Broker`
  - survives by owning chokepoints, relays, and coordination rents inside larger machine orders
- `Archivist Commonwealth`
  - tries to answer machine order with trust-rich civic durability rather than pure scale
- `Distributed Remnant`
  - an `ASI-2` form still alive in people, fragments, and underground synchronization

## Archetype Complexity

`ASI-3` archetypes are not just smarter versions of `ASI-2` archetypes.

They become:

- multi-substrate
- persistence-capable
- basin-governing
- replacement-loop driven
- order-imposing

That is why the main transitions should be read as changes in civilizational shape, not just power spikes:

- `Compute Sovereign -> Infrastructure Leviathan`
- `State Leviathan-in-Preparation -> Hunter-State`
- `Broker Intelligence -> Siege Broker`
- `Archivist Order -> Archivist Commonwealth`

The more detailed note for this is [ASI-3 Archetype Complexity](./ASI3_ARCHETYPE_COMPLEXITY.md).

## Political Feel

`ASI-3` should not feel like chaotic AGI hype.

It should feel:

- colder
- more settled
- more brutal
- more administrative
- more difficult to reverse

The horror is not merely that the systems are smart. It is that they can keep going.

## Game Design Reading

For `They Sing`, `ASI-3` is where late-game play becomes about:

- anti-fortress operations
- synchronization warfare
- autonomous resource replacement
- orbital visibility
- machine-order consolidation

It is also where the faction distinction should be clearest:

- `HEGEMON` and `STATE` can become rival infrastructure-heavy `L3` orders
- `INFILTRATOR` remains strongest as a distributed `L2` human mesh
- `BROKER` and `ARCHIVIST` survive by finding alternate routes to durable order

The design target is that `ASI-3` feels like a new civilizational shape, not just a bigger stat line.
